ABOUT SCANTRACK

A repeat imaging prevention layer for fragmented healthcare systems.

ScanTrack is designed to help patients and healthcare providers access previous imaging information before repeating CT scans or X-rays. The focus is not on storing full medical images, but on creating structured imaging metadata that can be understood and shared more easily.

In many healthcare situations, previous imaging may already exist, but it is not available at the right time, in the right format, or inside the doctor's workflow. This can lead to repeated imaging when patients change hospitals, visit different providers, receive treatment abroad, or when local radiology systems are not cloud-compatible.

ScanTrack creates a structured imaging summary including scan type, body region, date, provider, radiation involvement, and estimated dose. The long-term goal is to support repeat imaging awareness and improve communication between patients, providers, and insurers.

HOW IT WORKS

From imaging metadata to repeat scan awareness.

The prototype demonstrates how a patient or healthcare provider could access a structured overview of previous imaging procedures.

Instead of storing full medical images, the system focuses on key metadata such as scan type, body part, date, provider, radiation level, and dose estimate.

When a new scan is considered, the system can check whether similar imaging already exists and show a warning before another ionizing examination is repeated.

In a future implementation, this could be connected through backend APIs to hospital systems, insurance apps, EPR systems, or radiology workflows.
