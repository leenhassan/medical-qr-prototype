ABOUT SCANTRACK

A patient-owned imaging passport for modern healthcare.

ScanTrack is designed to help patients collect, organize, and understand their medical imaging history across different healthcare providers. Many patients receive CT scans, X-rays, MRI exams, or other imaging procedures at different clinics or hospitals, but this information is often stored separately in each system.

This fragmentation can make it difficult for patients and doctors to see a complete overview of previous imaging. As a result, scans may sometimes be repeated because earlier records are not easily available at the point of care.

ScanTrack gives patients a structured “Imaging Passport” where they can scan QR codes, add imaging records, view scan details, and understand basic radiation-related information. The goal is not to replace doctors, but to improve transparency, support better communication, and help patients become more aware of their own medical imaging history.

HOW IT WORKS

From QR scan to personal imaging history.

The ScanTrack experience begins with a simple and secure login process using a phone number and verification code. This ensures that each patient has access to their own private imaging history.

Once logged in, patients can scan a QR code attached to their imaging report. This QR code acts as a quick and easy way to input scan data into the app without manually typing information.

After scanning, the app stores the imaging record in the patient’s personal history. Over time, this builds a complete timeline of medical imaging procedures, allowing patients to review past scans, track frequency, and receive simple insights such as reminders or basic radiation awareness.

This process helps reduce unnecessary repeat imaging, improves communication with healthcare providers, and supports more informed decision-making without interfering with clinical judgment.
